# Pricing Page Copy Brief

Last updated: June 11, 2026

Reference influence:

- Dwight pricing section clarity
- existing WorkCV pricing-led positioning
- UK competitor billing comparison needs

This is a copy brief, not final page copy.

## Pricing page job

The pricing page exists to convert suspicion into action.

It must answer:

- what does WorkCV cost?
- when do I pay?
- is this a subscription?
- how does that compare with major alternatives?

## Primary conversion goal

- click into the editor flow

## Secondary conversion goal

- click to comparison pages

## Core promise

`GBP 4.99 once. Nothing after that.`

## Tone

- direct
- factual
- calm
- slightly sharper than the homepage
- no clever pricing copy that obscures the offer

## Messaging hierarchy

### Level 1

- one-time GBP 4.99 payment

### Level 2

- no subscription
- no automatic renewal
- build free first

### Level 3

- category comparison with validated competitor pricing

## Page structure

### Section 1: Hero

Job:

- state the price plainly
- frame the pain of recurring billing

Eyebrow direction:

- `No monthly fees`

H1 direction:

- `GBP 4.99 once. Nothing after that.`
- alternative: `A CV builder price that stays simple.`

Subheading direction:

- `Build your CV free. Pay GBP 4.99 when you download the final PDF. No subscription, no automatic renewal, and nothing to cancel later.`

Primary CTA:

- `Build my CV for GBP 4.99`

Secondary CTA:

- `Compare builders`

### Section 2: Pricing summary card

Job:

- make the commercial model visually undeniable

Core elements:

- price: `GBP 4.99`
- label: `one-time payment`
- points:
  - build free first
  - pay when downloading
  - no subscription
  - no automatic renewal
  - same paid CV can be edited and downloaded again later

### Section 3: Competitor comparison table

This is the key commercial section.

Job:

- make trial-to-renewal pricing legible
- show the economic difference in one scan

Competitors to include:

- MyPerfectCV
- LiveCareer UK
- CVMaker UK
- optionally Resume.io only if the currency/UK-localization caveat is clearly stated

Rows to include:

- entry price
- renewal price
- billing cadence
- auto-renews
- cancellation needed
- displayed currency

Required note:

- `Prices checked June 11, 2026. Always verify current pricing on the official site.`

### Section 4: Why this model fits one-CV users

Job:

- explain that the advantage is not just lower cost, but better fit

H2 direction:

- `Most people do not need a monthly CV platform.`

Support points:

- many users only need one strong CV for a current round of applications
- recurring billing is often a mismatch for that job
- WorkCV is intentionally designed around completion, not long-term platform usage

### Section 5: Comparison-page links

Job:

- route comparison-intent users deeper without losing them

Links to feature:

- MyPerfectCV alternative
- LiveCareer alternative
- CVMaker UK alternative

### Section 6: FAQ

Required questions:

- How much does WorkCV cost?
- Is WorkCV a subscription?
- When do I pay?
- Can I build my CV before paying?
- Can I edit my CV later?
- Why is WorkCV cheaper than subscription-led builders?

Answer rule:

- answer plainly
- keep billing language exact
- do not imply broader product scope than exists

### Section 7: Final CTA

H2 direction:

- `Pay once. Download your CV. Move on.`

Subtext direction:

- `Build free first. Pay GBP 4.99 only when you want the final PDF.`

Primary CTA:

- `Build my CV`

## Dwight-inspired rules to keep

- strong section order
- pricing clarity early
- low-noise design
- concise copy blocks with clear headings

## Rules not to break

- no unsupported complaint claims
- no stale competitor prices
- no hedgy copy around the actual price
- no large feature wishlist on the pricing page

## Pricing page success criteria

A visitor should leave the page knowing:

- the exact WorkCV price
- that it is not a subscription
- when payment happens
- why the model is simpler than the main alternatives
