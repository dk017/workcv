# Homepage Copy Brief

Last updated: June 11, 2026

Reference influence:

- Dwight homepage structure and tone: strong hero, clear problem/solution section, compact trust signals, direct CTA flow, FAQ near the bottom, restrained SaaS polish

This is a copy brief, not final page copy.

## Homepage job

The homepage must do five things quickly:

1. explain what WorkCV is
2. explain why the pricing model is different
3. prove UK relevance
4. reduce suspicion around subscriptions
5. move the user into the editor

## Primary conversion goal

- click into the editor flow

## Secondary conversion goal

- click into pricing

## Core promise

`Build your CV free. Pay GBP 4.99 when you download. No subscription.`

## Tone

- calm
- sharp
- commercial
- specific
- not hypey
- not chatty
- not American resume-marketing language

## Messaging hierarchy

### Level 1

What this is:

- a UK CV builder

### Level 2

Why it is different:

- one-time GBP 4.99 payment
- no subscription
- no automatic renewal

### Level 3

Why it is credible:

- built around UK CV norms
- simple builder flow
- practical templates

## Page structure

### Section 1: Hero

Job:

- establish the offer in one screen
- remove pricing ambiguity
- show UK relevance fast

Suggested eyebrow:

- `UK CV builder`
- or `No subscription · Pay once · Download when ready`

H1 direction:

- `Build a UK CV without a subscription.`
- alternative: `Your CV. Ready without the monthly fees.`

Subheading direction:

- `Create your CV free, then pay GBP 4.99 when you download the final PDF. Built for UK job applications, with clean templates and no recurring billing.`

Hero bullets:

- `UK-ready format`
- `One-time GBP 4.99 download`
- `No automatic renewal`

Primary CTA:

- `Build my CV`

Secondary CTA:

- `See pricing`

Proof requirement:

- the hero must not claim `no account needed`
- the hero must not claim user counts or ratings

### Section 2: Pricing trust strip

Job:

- repeat the commercial difference before the user scrolls into detail

Message direction:

- `Build free`
- `Pay GBP 4.99 when you download`
- `No subscription`
- `No automatic renewal`

### Section 3: Problem / solution comparison

This is the most important Dwight-inspired section.

Job:

- frame the category pain clearly
- make WorkCV feel simpler, not just cheaper

Section label direction:

- `The problem`

H2 direction:

- `Most CV builders charge you monthly for something you may only use once.`

Comparison framing:

Typical CV builder:

- low trial price
- recurring renewal
- cancellation required
- broader platform than many users need

WorkCV:

- build free
- pay GBP 4.99 once
- no subscription
- focused on finishing one strong CV

Primary CTA:

- `Build my CV`

### Section 4: How it works

Job:

- reduce friction
- make the flow feel short and controlled

Suggested 3-step structure:

1. Fill in your details
2. Choose a clean UK-ready template
3. Pay GBP 4.99 and download when ready

Copy requirement:

- keep each step short
- do not over-explain the account/login step here
- the goal is momentum, not policy detail

### Section 5: Template/sample preview

Job:

- show that the output looks clean and practical
- reinforce UK relevance

Message direction:

- `Clean templates for UK job applications`
- `Built to be readable, practical, and easy to tailor`

CTA:

- `See templates`

### Section 6: UK fit section

Job:

- prove the product understands UK CV expectations

Suggested H2 direction:

- `Built around how UK CVs are expected to work`

Points to support:

- no photo-first assumptions
- no date of birth or nationality prompts in the standard flow
- practical structure for early-career and experienced users
- clear PDF output for applications

### Section 7: FAQ

Job:

- close the last trust gaps before conversion

Required questions:

- How much does WorkCV cost?
- Is WorkCV a subscription?
- Do I need to log in before using the editor?
- Is the format right for UK jobs?
- Can I edit my CV later?

Answer rule:

- answer directly
- no filler
- no exaggerated claims

### Section 8: Final CTA

Job:

- give one clean final route into the editor

H2 direction:

- `Build your CV without the monthly fees.`

Subtext direction:

- `Start free. Pay GBP 4.99 when you download. No subscription.`

Primary CTA:

- `Build my CV`

## Dwight-inspired rules to keep

- strong hero with very clear value proposition
- one standout comparison section
- compact, high-signal section flow
- pricing clarity in multiple places
- minimal CTA sprawl

## Dwight-inspired rules not to copy blindly

- do not import their exact SaaS wording style
- do not add enterprise-looking complexity
- do not stack too many feature blocks
- do not use vague platform language when a direct commercial statement works better

## Homepage success criteria

A user should understand these points within seconds:

- this is for UK CVs
- it is not a subscription
- they build free and pay GBP 4.99 when downloading
- it is meant to finish one strong CV fast
